San Francisco Arts Commission


The SF Arts Commission is the city council that champions the arts. Through art education programs, street activation initiatives, public art work, grant funded performances and more, the SFAC acts as a catalyst for life-changing experiences for the citizens and tourists of one of the most beautiful cities in the world.

Citizens connect with the public art commissioned by SFAC for several intentions:

Our Mission


The SFAC is looking to be much more transparent in it's plans for public art, and wants to engage the city's inhabitants to help prioritize what it invests in.

Our Proposed Solution


We modeled designs for an accessible turnable public voting kiosk which can hold a digital touch device like an iPad which as a public option polling application. These kiosks are located in public spaces in the city where citizens pause.

Video of voting application prototype:
Arriving at a Strategy


From our research and collaborative design process, we realized that the best way to engage public participation is by intervening seamlessly in public spaces where citizens might pause.

Since citizens interface with the city at several touchpoints like public areas, this gives SFAC more opportunities to connect with people to anonymously vote for at while they pass by or wait...

Research for Design:


Our design proposal is based on user research, competitive and comparitive analyses. Our design hypotheses and solution statements were born of surveys, interviews and prototypes, which are described here.

Competitive Analysis

Personas
